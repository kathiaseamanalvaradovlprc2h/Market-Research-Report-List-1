<p><p>Xenon test chambers are available in two main types based on cooling methods: Air Cooling and Water Cooling. In the Air Cooling market, chambers use air as a medium to remove heat generated during testing. This is a cost-effective option suitable for applications where precise temperature control is not critical. On the other hand, Water Cooling chambers use water to efficiently dissipate heat, making them ideal for tests that require strict temperature control and where heat dissipation is a major concern.</p></p>
