<p><p>Lithium ion battery cathode materials market types include cobalt, manganese, nickel cobalt manganese (NMC), and lithium iron phosphate (LFP). Cobalt is commonly used for its high energy density but is expensive and controversial due to ethical concerns. Manganese is lower in cost but has a lower energy density. NMC combines nickel, cobalt, and manganese for a balance of performance and cost. LFP is known for its safety and stability but has lower energy density. Each material type offers different trade-offs in terms of performance, cost, and environmental impact.</p></p>
