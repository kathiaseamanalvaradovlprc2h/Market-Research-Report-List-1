<p><p>Backup power systems can be classified into two main types based on the type of current they operate on - AC and DC markets. AC backup power systems are designed to work with alternating current, commonly found in residential and commercial buildings. On the other hand, DC backup power systems are designed to work with direct current, which is commonly used in telecommunications, data centers, and other applications requiring a more stable and reliable power source. Both types of backup power systems serve the purpose of providing emergency power during outages or disruptions in the main power supply.</p></p>
