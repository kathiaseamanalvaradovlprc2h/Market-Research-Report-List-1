<p><p>Deli meat market types can be classified into two categories: cured and uncured. Cured deli meats undergo a preservation process using salt, nitrates, and other additives to enhance flavor and extend shelf life. On the other hand, uncured deli meats are made without synthetic nitrates or nitrites, relying instead on natural ingredients like celery juice or sea salt for preservation. Both types offer a wide variety of options for consumers looking for convenient, ready-to-eat meats for sandwiches, salads, and charcuterie boards.</p></p>
