<p><p>Network-attached storage (NAS) systems are available in two main market types: on-premise and cloud-based. On-premise NAS involves storing data on dedicated hardware within a company's physical premises, providing more control over security and performance. On the other hand, cloud-based NAS utilizes third-party servers for data storage and management, offering scalability and flexibility. Both markets cater to different business needs, with on-premise NAS being favored for data-sensitive industries and cloud-based NAS for its cost-effective and convenient storage solutions.</p></p>
