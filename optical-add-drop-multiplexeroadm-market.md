<p><p>An Optical Add Drop Multiplexer (OADM) is a device used in optical communication networks to add or remove specific wavelengths of light signals. There are two main types of OADMs: Fixed OADMs, which have a fixed number of add and drop ports for specific wavelengths, and Reconfigurable OADMs, which offer flexibility in adding and dropping wavelengths by dynamically configuring the ports. Fixed OADMs are more cost-effective and simpler to manage, while Reconfigurable OADMs offer greater flexibility and scalability in network design.</p></p>
