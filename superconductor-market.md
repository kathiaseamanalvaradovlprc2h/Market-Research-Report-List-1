<p><p>Superconductors can be classified into two main types based on the temperature at which they exhibit superconducting properties: Low-Temperature Superconductors (LTS) and High-Temperature Superconductors (HTS). LTS operate at very low temperatures, close to absolute zero, while HTS operate at higher temperatures, although still significantly below room temperature. LTS have been used longer and are more established in industries such as medical devices, while HTS are newer and offer higher performance but at a higher cost. Both types have unique applications and market opportunities.</p></p>
